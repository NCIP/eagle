F-test: One Way ANOVA Details
When you select the F-test One Way ANOVA option to test a hypothesis of the means of two or more populations, the technique is called the Analysis of Variance (ANOVA). The ANOVA simplifies the F-test, where F-test is the mean square for each main effect and the interaction effect divided by the within variance. A one-way ANOVA tests differences between the groups classified only on one independent variable. Using ANOVA instead of multiple t-tests reduces the probability of a type-I error.
