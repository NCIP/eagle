ANOVA Details
In statistics, ANalysis Of VAriance (ANOVA) is a collection of statistical models, and their associated procedures, in which the observed variance is partitioned into components due to different explanatory variables. F-distribution is used for statistical significance in ANOVA. Multivariate analysis of variance is an extension of analysis of variance (ANOVA) methods to cover cases where there is more than one dependent variable and where the dependent variables cannot simply be combined. As well as identifying whether changes in the independent variables have a significant effect on the dependent variables, the technique also seeks to identify the interactions among the independent variables and the association between dependent variables, if any. Using our analysis application of ANOVA user can easily determine the confounding variables by studying the change of p value.
