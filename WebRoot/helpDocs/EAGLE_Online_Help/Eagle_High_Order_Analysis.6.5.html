GLM or Generalized Linear Model Details
A Generalized Linear Model is a linear regression model of the least squares for multiple dependent variables. It works on non-normal data and is more flexible than a regular linear model. For a regular linear model, there is μ = Xβ. Instead of using μ, GLM uses g(μ) = Xβ, where g() is usually a known monotonic function. This processing makes regression modeling more reliable and insensitive to the range of dependent variable.
Most of time, g() is a logarithm function. In multivariate regression, the effect of each variable on the right of the equal symbol can be captured by the model. Sometimes, the variables are not independent of each other, which means that a single variant regression can be significant when combined with other variables. The significance could be overlooked. GLM helps identify the most dominant variables and assists in revealing the significance after the adjustment of the known associated covariates.
